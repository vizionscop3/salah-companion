# **1\. Executive Summary**

Salah Companion is a comprehensive mobile application designed to bridge the gap between ritual recitation and spiritual connection for Muslims learning to pray. Unlike existing prayer apps that treat Salah as a checklist, this application addresses the core struggles faced by new Muslims and those seeking deeper practice: understanding Arabic pronunciation, comprehending the meaning of what they recite, and building genuine connection with Allah through informed worship.

The application serves intentional practitioners—those who have consciously chosen Islam or chosen to deepen their inherited faith—by providing comprehensive pronunciation pedagogy, beautiful Azan notifications, real-time prayer guidance, AI-powered recitation feedback, gamified learning for accessibility, complete prayer time management, and Islamic calendar education rooted in Quranic text and authentic hadiths.

**Key Differentiators:**

* Beautiful Azan playback with education on meaning and response  
* AI-powered recitation practice with real-time pronunciation correction  
* Gamification system designed for accessibility and learning disabilities  
* Complete Islamic calendar with holiday guidance and conduct education  
* Progressive learning that builds understanding, not just memorization  
* Offline-first architecture for global accessibility

# **2\. Problem Statement**

## **2.1 The Pronunciation Barrier**

New Muslims face a significant linguistic barrier when learning to pray. Arabic is a phonetically complex language with sounds that don't exist in English (such as 'ayn, ghayn, and emphatic letters). Existing apps provide transliterations but fail to teach *how* to produce these sounds correctly. Users are left copying sounds they've never been trained to make, leading to frustration and self-doubt about the validity of their prayers.

## **2.2 Recitation Without Understanding**

Many Muslims—both new and born—recite prayers without understanding what they're saying. This creates a disconnect between the physical act of prayer and its spiritual purpose. When you don't know that "Al-hamdu lillahi Rabbil 'alamin" means "All praise is due to Allah, Lord of all the worlds," the words become sounds without soul. True khushu' (spiritual presence in prayer) requires comprehension.

## **2.3 No Practice Feedback Loop**

Current apps provide audio recitations to listen to, but listening alone doesn't build muscle memory or correct pronunciation habits. Users hear the correct sound but have no way to verify if their own attempt matches. They practice in isolation, uncertain if they're reinforcing correct pronunciation or ingraining mistakes. Not every Muslim has access to a qualified Qari or tajweed teacher.

## **2.4 Learning Accessibility Gaps**

Muslims with learning disabilities, ADHD, dyslexia, or cognitive challenges are underserved by existing apps. Traditional text-heavy interfaces and linear learning paths create barriers. These believers need alternative engagement methods—visual progress, achievement systems, bite-sized challenges, and multiple pathways to success—that make learning prayer accessible and even enjoyable.

## **2.5 Disconnection from Islamic Calendar**

New Muslims often feel lost when Islamic holidays arrive. When is Ramadan? What do I do during Eid? How should I conduct myself during Ashura? Existing apps may show dates but fail to educate users on the significance, rituals, and proper conduct for each occasion—leaving new believers feeling excluded from communal Islamic life.

# **5\. Core Features — Prayer Guidance**

### **Letter Introduction Module**

* Visual diagrams showing tongue placement, lip shape, and airflow  
* Native speaker audio for each letter (male and female voices)  
* Comparison with similar English sounds where applicable

* **Familiar sounds:** Letters with English equivalents (b, t, m, n, etc.)  
* **Modified sounds:** Letters requiring adjustment (h vs. ح, s vs. ص)  
* **Unique sounds:** Letters with no English equivalent (ع, غ, خ, ق)  
* **Emphatic letters:** Heavy consonants that affect surrounding vowels (ط, ظ, ض)

* Progress from letters to syllables to words  
* Color-coded transliterations highlighting trouble spots  
* Syllable-by-syllable breakdown with tap-to-hear

# **6\. Core Features — Azan (Call to Prayer)**

The Azan (أذان) is the Islamic call to prayer, announced at the beginning of each prayer time. Salah Companion provides a comprehensive Azan feature that not only plays beautiful recitations but educates users on its meaning and teaches the proper response.

## **6.1 When the Azan is Called**

The Azan is called at the **beginning** of each prayer time to announce that the time for prayer has entered.

| Prayer | When Azan is Called | Notes |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Fajr** | At true dawn (white horizontal light on horizon) | Includes "Prayer is better than sleep" |
| **Dhuhr** | When sun passes its zenith (begins to decline) | Standard Azan |
| **Asr** | Shadow equals object's length (Shafi'i) or twice (Hanafi) | Standard Azan |
| **Maghrib** | Immediately after sunset | Called promptly; shorter window |
| **Isha** | When red twilight disappears | Standard Azan |

## **6.2 Azan Audio Playback**

### **Voice Options**

* **Makkah Azan:** Sheikh Ali Ahmed Mulla (Masjid al-Haram, Makkah)  
* **Madinah Azan:** Sheikh Essam Bukhari and others (Masjid an-Nabawi)  
* **Al-Qatami:** Sheikh Nasser Al-Qatami  
* **Alafasy:** Sheikh Mishary Rashid Alafasy  
* **Custom:** Import personal recordings

### **Playback Settings**

* Full Azan, short Azan, or notification tone only  
* Volume control independent of device volume  
* Fade-in option for gentle wake (especially for Fajr)  
* Duration control (full length or abbreviated)  
* Different voice for Fajr option (to distinguish dawn prayer)  
* Vibration pattern before audio begins

### **Smart Scheduling**

* Automatic Do Not Disturb override option  
* Weekday vs. weekend settings  
* Location-aware (different settings for home, work, mosque)  
* Silent mode during detected meetings (calendar integration)  
* Gradual volume increase option

## **6.4 Responding to the Azan**

Education on the Sunnah response when hearing the Azan.

### **During the Azan**

* Repeat each phrase after the muezzin  
* When you hear "Hayya 'ala as-Salah," say: "La hawla wa la quwwata illa billah" (There is no power nor strength except with Allah)  
* When you hear "Hayya 'ala al-Falah," say: "La hawla wa la quwwata illa billah"  
* For Fajr, when you hear "As-Salatu khayrun min an-nawm," repeat the phrase

### **After the Azan**

Recite the dua after Azan:

اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ هَذِهِ الدَّعْوَةِ التَّامَّةِ وَالصَّلَاةِ الْقَائِمَةِ آتِ مُحَمَّدًا الْوَسِيلَةَ وَالْفَضِيلَةَ وَابْعَثْهُ مَقَامًا مَحْمُودًا الَّذِي وَعَدْتَهُ

*Allahumma Rabba hadhihi'd-da'watit-tammah was-salatil-qa'imah, ati Muhammadan al-wasilata wal-fadilah, wab'athhu maqaman mahmuda nilladhi wa'adtah*

"O Allah, Lord of this perfect call and established prayer, grant Muhammad the intercession and favor, and raise him to the honored station You have promised him."

# **7\. Core Features — Recitation Practice & Correction**

The Recitation Practice & Correction System enables users to record their recitation and receive real-time, AI-powered feedback on pronunciation accuracy. This feature transforms passive listening into active practice, allowing believers to refine their tajweed and build confidence.

## **7.1 Practice Modes**

### **Word-by-Word Mode (Beginner)**

* Single word displayed with Arabic, transliteration, and translation  
* Tap to hear correct pronunciation (multiple speeds)  
* Record button to capture user's attempt  
* Instant feedback with visual and audio comparison  
* Phoneme-level breakdown showing which sounds need work

### **Ayah Mode (Intermediate)**

* Full ayah (verse) displayed  
* Record user's full recitation  
* Word-by-word analysis highlighting accurate vs. needs-improvement  
* Tap any highlighted word to drill down into specific feedback  
* Overall score with breakdown by category

### **Full Surah Mode (Advanced)**

* Continuous recording through entire surah  
* Real-time visual cues showing current position (karaoke-style)  
* Post-recitation comprehensive analysis  
* Timestamped markers for areas needing attention  
* Comparison playback: user vs. reference side-by-side

## **7.2 Feedback System**

### **Visual Feedback**

* **Color-coded accuracy:** Green (excellent), Yellow (acceptable), Orange (needs work), Red (significant error)  
* **Waveform visualization:** User's audio waveform overlaid on reference waveform  
* **Phoneme highlighting:** Specific letters/sounds that differ from reference  
* **Progress rings:** Visual representation of improvement over time

### **Audio Feedback**

* **Side-by-side playback:** Hear reference, then user's attempt, then reference again  
* **Isolated problem areas:** Clip and loop specific sounds that need attention  
* **Slow-motion comparison:** Slowed audio to hear subtle differences  
* **Voice coaching prompts:** "Try placing your tongue further back"

### **Written Feedback**

* **Specific corrections:** "The letter 'ayn should come from deep in the throat"  
* **Tajweed rule explanations:** "This is an example of idgham—the noon should merge"  
* **Encouragement:** "Your pronunciation has improved 23% this week\!"  
* **Links to learning modules:** "Review the lesson on emphatic letters"

## **7.3 Tajweed Analysis**

Beyond basic pronunciation, the system analyzes adherence to tajweed rules.

* **Makharij al-Huruf:** Correct articulation points for each letter  
* **Sifat al-Huruf:** Characteristics of letters (heavy/light, voiced/unvoiced)  
* **Noon and Meem rules:** Ikhfa, idgham, iqlab, izhar  
* **Madd (elongation):** Proper lengthening of vowels  
* **Qalqalah:** Echoing sound on certain letters  
* **Ghunnah:** Nasalization  
* **Waqf and Ibtida:** Proper stopping and starting points

# **10\. Gamification & Accessibility System**

A comprehensive gamification system designed not just for engagement, but specifically to support believers with learning disabilities, ADHD, dyslexia, and cognitive challenges. The goal is to make learning prayer accessible, rewarding, and joyful for everyone.

## **10.1 Design Philosophy**

Gamification in Salah Companion is rooted in Islamic values:

* **Consistency over intensity:** Celebrating small steps mirrors the hadith "The most beloved deeds to Allah are those done consistently, even if small"  
* **Joy is worship:** Learning is a form of worship; joy in learning honors this  
* **Multiple pathways:** Every believer has a unique path; the app supports all of them  
* **Accessibility first:** Features specifically designed for those who struggle with traditional learning  
* **No competition with others:** Never compare users to others; only personal growth matters

## **10.2 Achievement System**

### **Prayer Achievements**

* **First Step:** Complete your first guided prayer  
* **Seven Days of Light:** Pray all five daily prayers for 7 consecutive days  
* **Month of Devotion:** Pray all five daily prayers for 30 consecutive days  
* **Dawn Riser:** Pray Fajr on time for 7 consecutive days  
* **Beyond the Obligatory:** Complete all Sunnah prayers for a day

### **Learning Achievements**

* **The Opening:** Memorize Surah Al-Fatiha  
* **Building Blocks:** Learn 5 short surahs  
* **Voice of the Quran:** Complete Arabic letter pronunciation module  
* **Heeding the Call:** Learn the meaning and response to Azan  
* **Knowledge Seeker:** Complete the Foundations learning track

### **Recitation Practice Achievements**

* **First Recording:** Record your first recitation  
* **Precision:** Achieve 80%+ accuracy on any surah  
* **Dedicated Learner:** Practice for 7 consecutive days  
* **Breakthrough:** Show measurable improvement in pronunciation

## **10.3 Accessibility Features**

### **For Users with ADHD**

* Bite-sized learning chunks (5-10 minutes max)  
* Frequent rewards and acknowledgment  
* Visual timers showing session length
